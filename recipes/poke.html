Spicy Ahi Poke

Home

An infamous Hawaiian dish featuring cubed pieces of raw tuna tossed in a mouth-watering spicy sauce.

In Hawaiian, poke directly translates to "to slice" or "cut into pieces". The most traditional variation of this dish features cubed pieces of raw fish with sea salt, kukui nuts, and limu. With waves of immigrants from China and Japan during the plantation boom, other ingredients were introduced to the recipe including soy sauce and sesame oil. My favorite version of this recipe is one that mixes mayo and sriracha together to create a creamy and decadent sauce that coats each piece of fish. Different types of fish can be used in this recipe, but I usually prefer leaner cuts of tuna or ahi. After marinating for a little bit, this poke pairs perfectly with a piping hot bowl of rice or even by itself as a snack.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb tuna
  • 3 tbsp kewpie mayo
  • 2-3 tbsp sriracha
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp tobiko
  • 1/4 yellow onion - thinly sliced
  • green onions - thinly sliced
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Chill all ingredients - except for soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt
  2. Use a paper towel to pat the tuna dry and cut into 1/2 inch cubes. Set aside in a bowl.
  3. Add all ingredients listed in the order above to the bowl with the tuna and mix until thoroughly combined
  4. Serve on top of rice or by itself and enjoy!
