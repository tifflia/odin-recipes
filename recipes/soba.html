Cold Soba Noodles

Home

A traditional Japanese dish with buckwheat noodles, served cold.

During my second trip to Japan in the summer of 2023, I got to try these noodles in Kyoto at a restaurant that was overlooking Katsura River. Perhaps it was the scenery or the shock of being in such a beautiful place that made the noodles taste so much better, but I have been obsessed with this dish ever since. When cooked right, the bounce of the chewy buckwheat noodles is insanely addicting paired with the light yet deeply umami tsuyu sauce. The beauty of this dish is in its simplicity and since all the components of the dish should be chilled, it is a classic summertime meal that is great for lazy cooking days.

Ingredients

  • 1 bundle of dried buckwheat noodles (soba)
  • 1 tbsp mentsuyu
  • 1 cup iced water
  • shredded nori seaweed
  • green onions - thinly sliced
  • wasabi (optional)
  • grated daikson

Steps

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions, stirring regularly while boiling to avoid sticking.
  2. Drain soba in a sieve and rinse off excess starch under cold running water.
  3. Transfer the noodles to a large bowl of iced water. Chill for 30 seconds, drain well, and set aside.
  4. Prepare dipping sauce by mixing mentsuyu and iced water in a small cup, adjusting amounts to taste.
  5. For toppings, add sliced green onions, a dab of wasabi, and a ball of grated daikon for toppings to a small plate
  6. Arrange soba on a larger plate and sprinkle shredded nori on top.
  7. Serve the soba, dipping sauce, and toppings together and enjoy!
